120 degree Goniotomy Navigation System (GNS)

The integration of computer-assisted technology to assist the surgeons seamlessly remains a critical challenge. Addressing this, we present the deep learning (DL) based goniotomy navigation system (GNS), a pioneering tool for real-time guidance in minimally invasive glaucoma surgery.
Trained on a substantial dataset comprising 5 million frames from 186 anonymized videos from three medical institutions, the GNS is designed to complete two critical tasks: identifying 8 surgical phases of goniotomy (Task I), and tracking 10 different key instruments and anatomical structures (Task II). Each video was annotated by three independent ophthalmologists. The agreement between the audio alert outputs agreed upon by the expert panel and those predicted by the GNS was assessed utilizing ICC. The acceptability and usefulness of the GNS was assessed through post hoc survey collected from 12 glaucoma surgeons at varying expertise levels.

Algorithm Architecture

For Task I, we employed a Transformer-based method named End-to-End Long-form Online Action Detection (E2E-LOAD) network for its superior performance and code availability. In line with contemporary experimental protocols, we sampled frames from videos at a frequency of 24 frames per second (FPS) and structured the video into chunks of 6 frames. Decision-making occurred at the chunk level, allowing evaluation of surgical steps every 0.25 seconds.
In Task II, we employed the state-of-the-art real-time semantic segmentation network known as Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) Controller Network (PIDNet) . The input image was resized to 1536×1024 pixels during training, and model evaluation was conducted at the original resolution of 1920×1080 pixels.

Task Setting


8 Phases for Task I
  • Corneal incision by 3.2 mm keratome
  • Corneal incision by 15-degree
  • keratome Carbachol injection
  • OVD injection Gonioscopy observation
  • Incision of Schlemm's canal
  • OVD removal
  • Wound closure
  • Other
